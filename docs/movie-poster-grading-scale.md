# Movie Poster Grading Scale

**Important:** Movie poster grading is DIFFERENT from comic book grading (CBCS/CGC).
There is no single regulated authority — the industry uses a C1-C10 scale with letter equivalents.

## C1-C10 Grading Scale

| Grade | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **C-10** | Mint | Zero tolerance for flaws. "As new" with no signs of handling, no edge wear, no fingerprints. Extremely rare — very few posters warrant this grade. For rolled posters: no edge wear, corner dings, undulations, bends, or rippling. |
| **C-9** | Near Mint | Virtually unused with only the slightest signs of handling. Acceptable: up to 4 tiny pinholes in corners, slight color misregistration, very minor compression marks. For folded: slight fold wear that doesn't impact visual appeal. |
| **C-8** | Very Fine / Excellent | Slight signs of use but no significant wear. Acceptable: up to 10 small pinholes, minor creases under 1 inch, very slight color loss at folds, small tears under 1/2 inch. NO tape, punch holes, or heavy writing allowed. Also called "Condition A." |
| **C-7** | Fine / Very Good-Excellent | Light, normal wear — used but not abused. May include: light tape residue, creases, minor fold holes, minor fading, light writing, slight dampness staining. Transition grade between "nice" and "used." |
| **C-6** | Very Good | Typical used poster showing average wear. Acceptable: border damage, edge tears, stains, minor fold tears. Professional restoration permitted at this grade. |
| **C-5** | Good-Very Good | Heavily worn with multiple pinholes, staple holes, tape, tears, and soiling that exceed C-6 standards. |
| **C-4** | Good | Below-average showing heavy use. Missing border pieces, tape, writing, numerous pinholes, tears affecting overall appeal. |
| **C-3** | Fair | Heavily worn yet complete. Significant damage but poster is still intact. |
| **C-2** | Poor-Fair | Extremely worn, possibly incomplete. Very heavily used with damage requiring extensive restoration. |
| **C-1** | Poor | Lowest grade. Significant damage, possible brittleness. Valuable only as filler or restoration source. |

## Key Differences from Comic Book Grading

| Factor | Movie Posters | Comic Books (CBCS/CGC) |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Scale** | C-1 to C-10 (10 grades) | 0.5 to 10.0 (25+ grades with half-points) |
| **Authority** | No single regulated authority | CGC and CBCS are recognized authorities |
| **Folded vs Rolled** | Pre-1980s were ALL folded — factory folds are NOT defects | N/A |
| **Encapsulation** | Not common (posters are too large) | Standard practice (slabbed) |
| **Subjectivity** | More subjective, varies by dealer | More standardized |
| **Pinholes** | Common and expected (posters were displayed) | Not applicable |
| **Tape** | Allowed below C-8 | Major defect at any grade |
| **Restoration** | Linen-backing is common and accepted (can increase value) | Restoration must be disclosed, usually decreases value |

## Folded vs Rolled — Critical Note

- **Pre-1980:** ALL one-sheet posters were shipped to theaters FOLDED. Factory folds are NOT considered defects.
- **Post-1980s:** Posters began shipping rolled. Rolled posters are graded more strictly.
- **Linen-backing:** A professional preservation technique where the poster is mounted on linen. This is ACCEPTED and often INCREASES value for vintage posters (unlike comic restoration).

## What to Look For When Grading Posters

### Paper & Surface
- Brightness/fading of colors
- Toning (yellowing/browning from age)
- Foxing (brown spots from mold)
- Stains (water, coffee, etc.)
- Surface scuffs or scratches

### Edges & Borders
- Edge tears (length and into image area?)
- Border damage or missing pieces
- Corner wear/blunting
- Pinholes (count them — affects grade)
- Staple holes

### Folds
- Fold line wear/separation
- Color loss along fold lines
- Fold tears or holes at intersections
- Crossfold condition (where horizontal and vertical folds meet)

### Writing & Marks
- Writing on front (pen, marker, grease pencil)
- Writing on back
- Stamps (theater stamps, date stamps)
- Tape on front vs back
- Stickers

### Overall
- Completeness (is anything missing?)
- Restoration history (linen-backed? touch-ups?)
- Paper stock (original vs reproduction)
- Authentication markers (NSS numbers, printer codes, studio logos)
